# 🛡️ Securing Android Devices
Given below are some countermeasures that can help in protecting an Android device and the data stored on it from malicious users: 

1. **Enable Screen Lock** → Protects your phone from unauthorized access.
2. **Use Strong Passwords & Biometrics** → Secure device with a strong PIN, password, or pattern; use fingerprint or face unlock for extra safety.
3. **Never Root Device** → Prevents hackers from exploiting system-level access.
4. **Download Apps Only from Official Stores** → Reduces the risk of installing malware.
5. **Install Antivirus** → Use apps like Kaspersky to detect and block threats.
6. **Avoid Direct APK Downloads** → Prevents installation of malicious apps.
7. **Update OS Regularly** → Keeps security patches up to date.
8. **Use App Lockers** → Protects sensitive apps like messages, mail, or banking apps.
9. **Customize Locked Screen** → Display contact info for recovery if phone is lost.
10. **Enable Encryption** → Secures stored data from unauthorized access.
11. **Check App Permissions** → Ensure apps only request permissions they actually need.
12. **Create Multiple Accounts** → Protects privacy when sharing the device.
13. **Enable GPS Tracking** → Helps locate a lost or stolen device.
14. **Use Remote Wipe Tools** → Apps like Google Find My Device can erase stolen phone data.
15. **Disable Risky Features** → Turn off visible passwords, unused Wi-Fi, and insecure certificates.
16. **Uninstall Privacy-Invading Apps** → Removes apps that over-collect data.
17. **Use VPN** → Encrypts internet traffic to protect browsing and online activities.
18. **Block Ads** → Prevents malicious ads from injecting malware.
19. **Enable Two-Step Verification** → Adds an extra login security layer.
20. **Avoid SmartLock/Auto Sign-In** → Prevents easy bypass of authentication.
21. **Use Password Managers** → Apps like LastPass securely store complex passwords.
22. **Enable Screen Pinning** → Restricts users to a single app when sharing device.
23. **Check Vendor Security Support** → Buy phones that receive long-term updates.
24. **Backup Sensitive Data** → Store contacts and documents in the cloud for recovery.
25. **Restrict File Transfers** → Avoid unsafe USB connections to untrusted PCs/devices.
26. **Limit Personal Info Sharing** → Share only necessary details when signing up.
27. **Enable Google Play Protect** → Scans apps for suspicious behavior.
28. **Turn Off Bluetooth & NFC When Idle** → Prevents unauthorized connections.
29. **Disable USB Debugging When Not Needed** → Stops attackers from exploiting ADB access.
30. **Enable 2FA on Accounts** → Adds stronger protection for online accounts.
31. **Review Logs & App Activity** → Detects unusual behavior or hidden malware.

## Android Device Tracking Tools 
Android device tracking tools help you track and find the location of your Android device if it is lost, stolen, or misplaced. Attackers use these tools to track the location of the target mobile devices. Some widely used Android device tracking tools are listed below: 

- **Google Find My Device** [https://www.google.com] <br>
  Google Find My Device is a security feature that lets users locate, lock, or erase data from a lost or stolen Android device. It helps protect personal information by allowing remote actions such as factory reset, which deletes emails, contacts, photos, music, and other files from the device and SD card. This ensures sensitive data stays safe even if the device is not recovered.

To use Find My Device, your lost device must
- Be turned on
- Be signed in to a Google Account
- Be connected to mobile data or Wi-Fi
- Be visible on Google Play
- Have Location turned on
- Have Find My Device turned on 

To find, lock, or erase a lost or stolen device, follow the steps given below:
- Go to [https://www.google.com/android/find] and sign in to your Google Account.
- If you have more than one device, click the lost device at the top of the screen.
- The device gets a notification.
- On the map, see where the device is.
  - The location is approximate and might not be accurate.
  - If your device cannot be found, then you will see its last known location, if available.
- Pick what you want to do.
  - **Play Sound:** Rings your device at full volume for 5 minutes, even if it is set to silent or vibrate.
  - **Secure Device:** Locks your device with your PIN, pattern, or password. If you do not have a lock, you can set one. To enable someone to return your device to you, you can add a message or phone number to the lock screen.
  - **Factory Reset Device:** Permanently deletes all data on your device (but might not delete SD cards). Subsequently, Find My Device will not work on the device
