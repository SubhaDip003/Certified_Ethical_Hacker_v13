#  💥 Cloud Attacks  
In this section, we discuss various attack methods performed on the cloud computing environment. 

---
## Service Hijacking using Social Engineering 
Service hijacking happens when an attacker steals cloud credentials—by phishing, pharming, social engineering, or exploiting software flaws—and then uses those credentials to get into a CSP or customer account. Social engineering is nontechnical: attackers trick people (customers, IT staff, or CSP employees) into revealing passwords or resetting accounts, or they deploy keyloggers, guess weak passwords, or run cracking tools and phishing campaigns to capture credentials. Once inside, the attacker can read, change, or delete data and disrupt services, putting customer records, payment details, business plans, and staff identities at risk.
